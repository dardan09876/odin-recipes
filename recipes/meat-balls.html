Meat Balls

Meatballs are small, round portions of seasoned ground meat, typically beef, pork, or a combination of both, mixed with breadcrumbs, eggs, and spices. They are often shaped by hand and can be pan-fried, baked, or simmered in a sauce, such as marinara, for added flavor. Meatballs are versatile and can be enjoyed on their own or served with pasta, sandwiches, or in soups.

This dish is popular worldwide, with many variations reflecting different cultures and cuisines. From Italian-style meatballs in tomato sauce to Swedish meatballs served with gravy, each version features unique seasonings and preparation methods. Whether enjoyed as an appetizer or a main course, meatballs are loved for their savory, juicy texture and rich, comforting taste.

    Ingredients
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ pound ground veal
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • 1 cup freshly grated Romano cheese
  • 1 cup freshly grated Romano cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ tablespoons chopped Italian flat leaf parsley
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups stale Italian bread, crumbled
  • 1 ½ cups lukewarm water
  • 1 cup olive oil
    Steps
  1. Combine beef, veal, and pork in a large bowl. Mix in cheese, eggs, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add bread crumbs and slowly mix in water, 1/2 cup at a time, until mixture is moist but still holds its shape (I usually use about 1 1/4 cups of water); shape into meatballs.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet; add meatballs in batches and cook until browned on all sides, slightly crisp, and cooked through, about 10 to 15 minutes. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Dish up and enjoy!
